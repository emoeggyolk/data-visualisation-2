Malaysia’s Demographic Trends


In Malaysia, the rate at which people marry and
the number of births are closely intertwined — and they
vary significantly across states, by age, sex and ethnicity.
This visual story explores those relationships, answering:
Where and how do marriage patterns relate to birth trends?

Births per 1,000 Population by State, Malaysia (2022)

Where are babies being born in Malaysia?

As the map shows, birth rates vary
significantly across Malaysia, with states
like Terengganu having a much higher rate
than W.P. Kuala Lumpur.

This geographical view gives us the
'where,' but it doesn't explain the 'why.'
To understand the 'why' behind these numbers,
we first need to look at a key related trend:
marriage

Births vs Marriages in Malaysia (2017–2022)

How are these trends connected?

This chart shows that while marriages and
births are both key indicators of demographic
changes, the chart shows that these two metrics
are not always correlated. The decrease in births,
despite recovery in marriages, highlights broader
social changes and shifts in societal priorities
regarding family life.

What is the final proof?

The Proof:
States with More Marriages
Have More Births

This scatter plot provides the final evidence. Each dot represents a Malaysian state.
The clear upward trend shows a strong positive correlation: states with higher marriage rates also tend to have higher birth rates.
This confirms our story's central theme:
In Malaysia, marriage and birth remain
deeply intertwined demographic trends.
